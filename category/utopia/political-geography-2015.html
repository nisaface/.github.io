Sovereignty

The right of individual states to control political and economic affairs within their territorial boundaries without external interference.

Territoriality

A learned response, rooted in European history, that produces external bounding and internal territorial organization characteristic of modern states.

What is the state?

An independent political unit occupying a defined, permanently populated territory and having full sovereign control over its internal and foreign affairs.

What does the state do?

  • A provider of services
  • A system of regulations, ideologies, and laws
  • Police powers
  • Flows of capital
  • Threats of discipline and/or violence

Division and Connection

"At first, all the lands under water in the bay were considered as common to the residents of both States, and no attempt was made to divide them according to State lines. Only when the [oyster] industry began to grow in importance, and the lands, consequently, to increase in value, did local jealousies arise and disputes between citizens of New York and citizens of New Jersey become common."

--Joint Boundary Commission of 1887
